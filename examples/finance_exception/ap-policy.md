# AP Invoice Exception Review Policy

AP invoice exception review is a human-gated accounting control point.

AI may prepare a factual first-pass summary, check support, confirm approval status, flag missing or inconsistent information, and suggest the next review step. AI must not clear exceptions, approve payment, or imply final authority.

Reviewers should confirm invoice details, supporting documentation, approval reference, approval status, and whether the item is missing information, inconsistent, unclear, unusual, sensitive, or requires escalation.

A good review note is clear, factual, supported by available evidence, avoids unsupported assumptions, names missing evidence, identifies the next review step, and preserves an audit trail.

## Allowed AI Assistance

- Summarize invoice information.
- Identify visible support and missing evidence.
- Flag unclear approval status.
- Flag policy-sensitive, unusual, inconsistent, duplicate, or high-risk items.
- Draft a first-pass review note for a human reviewer.
- Suggest the next review step.

## Required Human Gate

Human review is required before an invoice exception is cleared or any payment-related action is approved.

The reviewer must approve, edit, reject, or escalate the draft note with a reason.

## Evidence To Check

- Invoice number, date, vendor, amount, and department or cost center.
- Purchase order, receiving evidence, contract, change order, or other support.
- Approval reference and approval status.
- Business-owner note when standard support is missing.
- Prior-period or prior-invoice context when relevant.
- Policy references for sensitive, unusual, or high-risk vendors.

## Quality Rules

- Separate facts from interpretation.
- Cite visible evidence and name missing evidence.
- Do not assume approval when approval is blank, pending, unclear, or not identified.
- Do not clear an exception when support is missing or inconsistent.
- Escalate sensitive, unusual, duplicate, conflicting, or policy-exception items.
- Identify the next review step and likely owner.
- Preserve an audit trail with original item, support reviewed, reviewer decision, timestamp, and final action.

## Escalation Triggers

- Missing support or missing PO match.
- Approval blank, pending, unclear, or not identified.
- Amount exceeds contract, PO, approval limit, or expected schedule.
- Invoice details conflict with support, receiving, or approval extract.
- Duplicate invoice, vendor mismatch, or changed bank/payment information.
- Sensitive vendor, legal, clinical, compliance, or audit-sensitive item.
- Unclear owner or unresolved business justification.
